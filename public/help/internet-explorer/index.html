Why doesn't Streetmix
support Internet Explorer?

While we're aware that Microsoft's Internet Explorer is the default browser in many large organizations, such as government agencies, Streetmix does not support any version of Internet Explorer right now. This is because Internet Explorer doesn't properly implement many of the modern web standards that are common in the competing browsers, such as Chrome, Firefox or Safari.

Technical details

Streetmix does not rely on any external plugins (like Java, Flash, Silverlight, etc). It uses only modern web standards.

Here are the following standards that Streetmix relies on, which Internet Explorer (depending on version) either does not support, or have been implemented with limited or buggy support. Clicking on each will provide more technical information about each feature, thanks to Can I Use?

  • Canvas
  • CSS pointer-events

In addition, Internet Explorer has a lot of bugs, quirks, or holes in its handling of the modern HTML/CSS/JavaScript specification. As a result, even if we were able to work around all of these, it would require an immense amount of time and effort.

Will Streetmix ever support Internet Explorer?

Based on our understanding of Internet Explorer's standards implementation, we believe that Internet Explorer 10 and 11 are likely candidates for Streetmix support at some point in the future, but we don't have a timeline of when that would occur yet. Because some of our most important features are not implemented in Internet Explorer 9 or lower, we will not be supporting those versions of Internet Explorer.

Isn't Internet Explorer 9 a modern browser?

Your organization might still be using it, but it's not a modern browser. Read this for more details.

Would supporting Internet Explorer increase our user base?

We're concerned that an organization's overwhelming reliance on old browsers will slow adoption of Streetmix, but Internet Explorer users are currently less than 8% of our visitors, much less than the worldwide browser share of 20%. We believe this is a sign that many organizations are transitioning and upgrading away from older versions of Internet Explorer.
